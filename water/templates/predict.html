Water Potability Prediction

💧 Prediction Overview

This module predicts whether water is safe for drinking using a trained Random Forest Machine Learning model. The system analyzes multiple chemical and physical parameters such as pH, solids, conductivity, and turbidity to determine potability. Users provide water characteristics and receive an instant classification result. The prediction helps support environmental monitoring and rapid decision-making. This feature demonstrates real-time ML deployment in a web application.

📘 Feature Explanation

pH — indicates acidity/alkalinity of water.
Hardness — mineral concentration affecting taste.
Solids — dissolved substances present in water.
Chloramines — disinfectant chemicals level.
Sulfate — affects taste and water quality.
Conductivity — measures ionic concentration.
Organic Carbon — organic pollutant indicator.
Trihalomethanes — chemical byproduct indicator.
Turbidity — water clarity measurement.

⭐ Feature Importance

Feature importance indicates which parameters most influence the prediction. Typically solids, sulfate, pH, and conductivity play major roles. Higher turbidity and abnormal chemical levels often lead to unsafe classification. Understanding feature importance improves model transparency and trust.
